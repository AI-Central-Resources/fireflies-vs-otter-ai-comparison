## 🔍 Tool Breakdowns

### Krisp
**Best for:** Teams bothered by background noise who also need basic meeting notes  
Krisp's primary product is AI-powered noise cancellation — it removes background sound from your microphone and speakers in real time. It also now includes AI meeting notes as a secondary feature. If your team works from noisy environments (open offices, home offices with kids, coffee shops), Krisp solves a problem the others don't.

**Limitation:** Meeting notes are not as comprehensive as Fireflies.ai or Otter.ai. Use Krisp if noise is your primary pain point.

---

### Jamie
**Best for:** Teams that can't or won't allow a recording bot in the call  
Jamie is notable because it doesn't inject a bot into your meetings. It records locally and generates summaries from the audio without a visible "Jamie is recording this meeting" notice. For teams with privacy-conscious clients or internal policies against recording bots, Jamie is the solution.

**Limitation:** Fewer integrations than Fireflies or Otter. Less powerful analytics.

---

### Fathom
**Best for:** Individuals and small teams who want a strong free tier on Zoom  
Fathom has one of the most generous free plans in the space — unlimited recording and AI summaries for Zoom meetings at no cost. It's a strong starting point for freelancers, consultants, or small teams who primarily use Zoom.

**Limitation:** Primarily Zoom-focused. Less robust for Google Meet or Teams users. Limited analytics.

---

### Avoma
**Best for:** Teams wanting a full meeting lifecycle tool (not just transcription)  
Avoma handles the entire meeting workflow: scheduling, agenda creation, live transcription, AI notes, post-meeting summaries, and coaching analytics. It's more of an end-to-end meeting intelligence platform than a pure transcription tool.

**Limitation:** Higher price point. More complexity than most individuals need.

---

### TwinMind
**Best for:** Personal productivity and AI memory across all conversations  
TwinMind runs locally and builds a personal memory across all your meetings, conversations, and even ambient sound if you choose. It's less about team collaboration and more about giving you an AI that remembers everything you've discussed.

**Limitation:** More personal productivity tool than team meeting tool. Limited team sharing features.

---

### Notta
**Best for:** Budget-conscious teams needing multilingual support  
Notta supports transcription across multiple languages at a lower price point than some competitors. It's a good alternative for teams that need Fireflies.ai-style multilingual support but aren't ready for Fireflies' pricing.

**Limitation:** Less conversation intelligence than Fireflies. Less real-time polish than Otter.

---

### Bluedot
**Best for:** Teams that want to record without a bot appearing in the meeting  
Bluedot uses a Chrome extension to capture meeting audio and video without sending a bot into the call. Participants don't see a "Bluedot is recording" bot on their participant list. Useful for sensitive client calls.

**Limitation:** Requires Chrome. Works best with Chromium-based browsers.

---

### Fellow.ai
**Best for:** Teams wanting structured agendas alongside meeting notes  
Fellow.ai is built around the concept of collaborative meeting agendas. You build the agenda before the meeting, take notes during it, and track action items after. It's great for structured recurring meetings like 1:1s, team standups, and executive reviews.

**Limitation:** Less AI-powered post-meeting intelligence compared to Fireflies.

---

### Zoom AI Companion
**Best for:** Organizations already paying for Zoom — it's included  
If your organization is on a Zoom plan that includes AI Companion, it's worth trying first before paying for a separate tool. It handles basic summaries and action items natively within the Zoom experience.

**Limitation:** Only works in Zoom. Limited analytics and no third-party integrations.
